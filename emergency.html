WHAT WE DO

Currently, we operate 155 schools in Northern Syria, making us the largest non-governmental education provider in the country. Our commitment extends beyond the present generation; we aspire to open doors of opportunity for Syrian children today and for generations to come.

In September 2023, we achieved a significant milestone by successfully building a new secondary school in an area of Northwestern Syria where many children, including 11-year-old Zayna, previously lacked access to secondary education.

PROVIDING EMERGENCY MEDICAL CARE

Approximately 12.2 million individuals in Syria are in dire need of essential healthcare services. The protracted crisis in the region has left a significant number of healthcare facilities in a state of disarray, rendering them either outdated or severely damaged. As a result, a substantial portion of the population struggles to access the crucial medical care they require.

WE'VE UNDERTAKEN THE FOLLOWING KEY AREAS OF ACTION:

  • Emergency Care: We are dedicated to providing immediate and life-saving emergency care to victims of the ongoing conflict. Our medical teams work tirelessly to address injuries and traumas resulting from the crisis, ensuring that timely medical attention is accessible to those in need.
  • Chronic Illness: In addition to emergency services, we recognise the importance of ongoing medical care. We offer regular healthcare support for individuals grappling with chronic illnesses.
  • Vaccination Programs: Preventative healthcare is a vital component of our initiative. We administer vaccination programs to safeguard the health of communities, particularly vulnerable groups such as children and the elderly. These programs play a crucial role in preventing the outbreak of infectious diseases.
  • Mental Health Support: The psychological toll of conflict is immense, and we understand the importance of addressing mental health concerns. As part of our program, we provide essential mental health support to help individuals cope with the emotional challenges they face in the wake of the crisis.

Syria Relief has consistently stood out as one of the most efficient and active humanitarian actors in Syria. For over three years, we've been at the forefront, managing critical healthcare facilities such as the Raqqa Maternity and Paediatric Hospital and the Shiheil Surgical Hospital in Deir ez-Zor. These facilities are lifelines for the communities they serve, collectively benefiting an average of 100,000 beneficiaries each year.

AHMAD'S STORY

Meet Ahmad, a 7-year-old boy residing in an IDP camp in Northwest Syria. Ahmad faced the challenge of hearing loss, which hindered his ability to communicate with his family and peers. However, thanks to the Syria Relief’s health team's visit to the camp, Ahmad received a life-changing gift—a pair of hearing aids that addressed his urgent need. With the newfound ability to hear clearly, Ahmad underwent a transformation. He engaged with his loved ones, actively participated in school activities, and felt a profound sense of belonging. His parents, overwhelmed with gratitude, expressed their appreciation, "Thank you to the generous donors for giving our son the gift of hearing through the hearing aids it has changed our life."

At Action For Humanity, our commitment to healthcare is unwavering, and our impact is measured not only in numbers but in the lives transformed and the hope restored to those we serve in Syria.

How You Can Help

Spreading Hope is a nonprofit organization dedicated to empowering Syrians to rebuild their lives and their communities through sustainable life-changing programs and services. Our comprehensive strategy includes education, training, job placement, economic empowerment, and research studies.

We also prioritize women empowerment and child protection, working to ensure that all Syrians have the support and resources they need to thrive and succeed.
